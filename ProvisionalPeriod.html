The Behind The Wheel Test

Congratulations on earning your driver's license! After you pass all the exams, and you are under 18, you are the owner of a provisional license. For exactly one year after you pass your behind-the-wheel exam, you must abide by certain driving restrictions. You cannot drive between 11 pm and 5 am. You cannot drive someone under 20 years of age unless a guardian, driving instructor, or licensed driver at least 25 years of age is in the vehicle.

If necessary, there are exceptions to this probation period. If you have a medical necessity, school-authorized activities, employment necessity, or an immediate need of a family member the DMV will make an exception. Here are the different methods to qualify for the exception:

  • Medical necessity:
  • The note must be signed by your physician Must include the date of recovery


  • School or school-authorized activities:
  • The note must be signed by your school principal, dean, or designee
  • Must include a reason for the school or school-authorized activity
  • Must have a probable date when the activity will end


  • Employment necessity:
  • The note must be signed by your employer, Must verify employment
  • Must include the probable date your employment will end.


  • Immediate need of family member:
  • The note must be signed by your parent or guardian
  • Must include the reason
  • Must include the probable date the necessity will end


The DMV's official Guide!

Breaking one of the provisional licensing rules can result in points being added to your driving record. If you have a violation point count of two or more within 12 months, you will receive a 30-day restriction. If you have three or more points within 12 months, you will receive a 6-month suspension and 1 year probation. Be a safe driver! If you would like to know more about the California DMV point system, check out their safety guidelines resource.
